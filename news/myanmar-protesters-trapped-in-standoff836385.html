A U.S. military spokesman said the troops were based in a military air base in the southern town of Tinh, about 60 miles (80 km) southwest of the capital.

"The U.S. military is supporting the government of Myanmar's government in the ongoing stand-off with the Burmese military, but will be ready to assist any defense or security assistance provided," the spokesman said. He did not give more details.

The United Nations had asked the international community to intervene to prevent a repeat of violence in the country, where the military says a minority Rohingya population has been fleeing violence.

The military says it has detained nearly 100 people, including some children, in the country over the past few weeks. It has also arrested around 150 people in connection with the violence.

The spokesman, Col. Tawasri Prawa, said military and political leaders were in talks with officials in the southern town of Tana, where the United Nations has warned that some 8,000 Rohingya have been displaced.

"We are still in discussions with the Thai government and Thai military," he said.

Rights groups said the clashes on the northern side of the border were being carried out by military, political and security forces who are "entirely out of control."

The violence was triggered after the military ousted a government that had helped create a Muslim majority in Myanmar and sought to establish its own state.

Rights groups said the killings were the first time that armed groups such as the Burmese army had attacked the ethnic group.
