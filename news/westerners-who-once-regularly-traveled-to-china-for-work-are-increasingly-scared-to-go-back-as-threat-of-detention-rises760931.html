Westerners who once regularly traveled to China for work are increasingly scared to go back as threat of detention rises.

Chinese authorities now have the ability to detain people without charge or trial if they have a history of violence, such as those who killed two Chinese tourists in Beijing last year.

They also can ask for medical and psychological help to combat their case, and could even bring back information about the suspects.

One of the most prominent cases, in which the government has sought to turn a blind eye to the abuse of detainees, involves a young man who was detained by the authorities last year on suspicion of being drunk, who had been arrested several times.

The government is now seeking to stop him from returning to China and trying to extradite him to the United States, where he will face charges of violent extremism.

The case comes after China's Supreme People's Court ordered a review of the case after Mr Zhao's lawyer, Yudong Wei, said he had filed for an appeal.
