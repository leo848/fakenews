Apple is discontinuing the iMac Pro

Apple is discontinuing the iMac Pro, the new MacBook Pro and the new MacBook Pro Ultra.

The iMac Pro is said to have been made available to Apple in December, but a source at Apple told Bloomberg the company is not stopping its plans to introduce the iMac Pro.

"Apple is discontinuing the iMac Pro, but it will do so in the future," the source said.

The new MacBook Pro and the MacBook Pro Ultra, while coming in two different capacities for the existing MacBook Pro, will sell for $200,000 to $300,000. The iMac Pro will cost $399, while the current MacBook Pro will be around $499.

Apple will also be selling the new MacBook Pro with a $300,000 discount on its stock.

The new MacBook Pro and the MacBook Pro Ultra are priced at $499, $599, $699, $799, $999 and $999 for the current and $399, $599 and $799, respectively.

Apple is also expected to announce next year's iPhone 5 and iPhone 5c.
