Republicans trust Fox News as their main source of information

In October, Fox News viewers reported that their trust of the network "fails to deliver on its promises to its core audience" as well as its commitment to air every two minutes an "interactive video on a wide range of topics of importance to all segments." The video, originally shown at the beginning of November 2015, revealed how Fox News would respond to attacks by its competitors.

With almost half of Fox News's audience tuning to "Fox News Sunday," which is live coverage of national events from inside the network, "Fox News Sunday" could easily make a "significant contribution" to the Republican primaries. For Fox News, "targeting and targeting these segments gives them the same sort of attention that viewers have been looking for. These segments only get to have those debates, which are critical, because they give a platform [to the candidates] which the broader public wants."
