Stimulus Check could hit millions of bank accounts next week

Stimulus Check could hit millions of bank accounts next week and could hit more than $1 billion in new accounts.

The move might cause a spike in the number of accounts available for customers.

'A huge hit'

The bank said it was not aware of any "significant" bank account hit that day with the move.

The move comes at a time when the U.S. Federal Reserve is struggling to raise interest rates, following a series of high bank-to-bank lending declines.

That has led to heightened interest rates and concerns about a possible economic meltdown if the economy does not improve.

The Bank of England said that "the impact of this move is enormous".

It added that "the impact of this credit expansion is likely to be greater than anticipated."

The move comes during a period when the U.K. is tightening monetary policy and the European Union is planning to open its doors to its citizens.
